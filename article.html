当身体开始说话：如何倾听你内在的智慧信号 When the body begins to speak: on listening to your inner wisdom

关于"放下"与"努力"的差别，关于身体一直在告诉你的那些事 —— 一段来自 Jack Kornfield 的冥想笔记。 On the difference between letting go and trying, and on what the body has been saying all along — a meditation note from Jack Kornfield.

Jack Kornfield 8 分钟阅读 8 min read · April 2025 · 译自 Sounds True Trans. Sounds True

很多人来到我的冥想课时，都以为"静下来"是一种努力的结果——需要强迫自己不去想事情，需要控制呼吸的节奏，需要"做对"某件事。但事实上，真正的静止，是一种放下，而不是努力。

Many people arrive at my meditation classes believing that "becoming still" requires effort — forcing yourself to stop thinking, controlling your breath, doing something "right." But true stillness is a releasing, not an achieving.

身体一直在说话 The body has always been speaking

在我们生命的每一刻，身体都在以各种方式传递信息。那种胃里的紧绷感，那种肩膀上不知从何而来的重量，那种莫名的疲惫……这些都不是"症状"，而是信号。只是我们大多数人已经学会了忽略它们。

In every moment of our lives, the body is communicating. That tightness in your stomach, the unexplained weight in your shoulders — these aren't symptoms. They're signals. Most of us have simply learned to ignore them.

"身体是你最诚实的老师。它从不撒谎——即使在你撒谎的时候，它也会诚实地告诉你正在发生什么。" "The body is your most honest teacher. It never lies — even when you do, it will tell you truthfully what is happening."

— Jack Kornfield
核心要点 Key Takeaways
  • 01身体的感觉是信号，不是需要被压制的症状。Bodily sensations are signals, not symptoms to suppress.
  • 02"静下来"不是努力，而是放下对控制的需求。Stillness isn't effort — it's releasing the need to control.
  • 03每天 5 分钟的身体扫描，可以重新建立头脑与身体的连结。Five minutes of daily body scan can rebuild the mind-body connection.

当身体说"不" When the body says "no"

有时候，倾听身体会带来一些不舒服的信息。你可能会发现自己其实不想去那个聚会，或者那段关系其实让你感到压抑，或者那份工作已经让你的身体疲惫到极点了。但正是这些"不舒服"，才是真正疗愈的开始。

Sometimes listening to the body brings uncomfortable messages. You might find you don't actually want to go to that party, that a relationship is draining you, or that work has pushed you past your limit. But it's precisely these uncomfortable truths where real healing begins.

学会倾听，意味着学会允许。允许那些一直被压抑的感觉浮现，允许身体以它自己的节奏说话 —— 而不是被你急切的 to-do list 推着往前走。这是一种练习：每天给自己五分钟，坐下，不做任何事，只是感受身体里正在发生的一切。

To listen is to allow — to let the sensations you have been suppressing rise into view, to let the body speak at its own pace, rather than be rushed by a to-do list. It's a practice: five minutes a day, seated, doing nothing, feeling whatever the body is feeling.
